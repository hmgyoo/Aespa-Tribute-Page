Here is a brief history of their success.

  • 2020: aespa debut, fandom name reveal

    On October 26, 2020, a video was posted on aespa's twitter which revealed the name of the group and their logo. SM Entertainment then went on to announce that they will be debuting their new girl group sometime in November.

  • 2021: "Forever", "Next Level", Savage, "Dreams Come True"

    On January 21, 2021, Cheon-hoon of Sonic Korea Mastering Studio teased aespa's comeback single, titled "Forever". The teaser was taken down a few hours after being posted. On January 29, 2021, aespa's official Twitter account promoted the hashtag #늘_네_옆에_난_서있을게 (#I_will_always_stand_by_your_side).

    On May 4th, SM Entertainment confirmed that aespa are preparing for a May comeback. The schedule for the comeback is set to be released once it's finalized. On May 6, a photo that said Next Level was posted on the girls' social media accounts. Later that day, it was confirmed that aespa will be releasing their third digital single, "Next Level" on May 17. Concept photos for "Next Level" were released on May 7 to May 15. During the press conference for "Next Level" it was revealed that they are planning on releasing their first full length album soon.

    On September 14, a short clip was posted onto the girls' social media accounts with the word Savage. About 12 hour laters, SM announced that aespa will be releasing their first mini album, Savage, on October 5. Concept photos were released starting on September 23.

  • 2022: GOT the beat, Coachella debut, Girls, and international expansion

    On December 27, members Karina and Winter were revealed as members of the first unit, GOT the beat, to the project group Girls On Top. The group debuted with the single "Step Back" on January 3, 2022.

    On April 19, 2022, SM Entertainment announced that Aespa would performing at the second week of the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, becoming the third K-pop girl group to do so.As part of their set, the group performed a previously unreleased English song, titled "Life's Too Short".

    Also on June 1, Aespa dropped a suprise lyric video for a new song called "Illusion", which will be featured on their upcoming mini album.

    On June 2, the group announced their first ever Japanese showcase tour, titled Aespa Japan Premium Showcase 2022 ~SYNK~, which will be held on August 6 and August 7 at Pia Arena MM in Yokohama, Japan.

    On June 6, it was announced that aespa will be holding their first ever showcase in the United States, 'SYNK' in LA! Aespa's first U.S. showcase will take place on June 26 at the YouTube Theater. Only one week later, it was announced that aespa's upcoming U.S. showcase 'SYNK IN LA' will have a second show because the tickets sold out completely.

    After some promotions in the United States for their first English single, Life's Too Short, and stunning teasers for their highly-anticipated korean comeback, the 2nd mini album and the music video for "Girls" was finally released on July 8, 2022. The mini album was a huge success and according to the Circle Chart's monthly update for July, aespa's 'Girls' ranked No. 1 in monthly sales with a total of 1,645,255 copies sold.
